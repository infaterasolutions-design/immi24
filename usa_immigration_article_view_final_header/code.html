New H-1B Selection Process: What Employers and Applicants Need to Know for 2024

Marcus Vance
Senior Immigration Analyst • Oct 24, 2024
Photography by USCIS Editorial Team. The new registration rules aim to reduce fraud in the lottery system.

The Department of Homeland Security has finalized a significant overhaul of the H-1B cap selection process. This change represents the most substantial shift in how high-skilled visas are allocated in over a decade.

Effective immediately for the upcoming fiscal cycle, the "beneficiary-centric" selection process replaces the old employer-centric lottery. Previously, an individual applicant's chances increased based on how many registrations were submitted on their behalf by different companies—a loophole that was often exploited.

"The goal is simple: to ensure that the lottery is fair and that every applicant has exactly one chance at being selected, regardless of how many job offers they have on the table."

Core Changes to the Registration System

Under the new rules, each unique beneficiary (applicant) will be entered into the selection process only once, based on their passport or travel document number. If an applicant is selected, every employer that submitted a valid registration for them will be eligible to file a full petition.

This prevents "stacking" where multiple entities would register the same person to artificially inflate selection odds. Analysts predict this will significantly lower the total number of registrations while increasing the "true" selection rate for legitimate candidates.

info Key Deadlines to Remember

  • • Registration Window: Opens March 1st at 12:00 PM ET.
  • • Account Setup: MyUSCIS accounts should be updated by Feb 15th.
  • • Fee Structure: The $10 registration fee remains in place for this cycle.

For employers, this means a shift in strategy. Instead of focusing on volume, HR departments should focus on ensuring their data integrity is flawless. Any discrepancy in the passport information across different registrations for the same beneficiary could lead to a disqualification of all entries.
